Daily Tasks

drive around first thing in the morning , look for visable problems:

  • flat tires
  • side steps damaged/bent
  • Netradyne cameras hanging loose
  • phone stands broken or missing
  • make a list for dispatch in the morning to state available Prime vans, vans out, and vans to hold back if possible

Weekly Tasks

  • Make/Update tire tread list (recommend only accounting for size 6 and below . Anything above a 6 is ok)
  • check for low coolant and windshield wiper fluid to refill (If needed buy more from Blains)
  • make a list of the back steps bent or loose also with sidesteps (physically stepping on them and checking if they are loose)
  • charge power tool batteries
  • turn on and check each van for working headlights to fix/replace with new ones (H7 lightbulbs)
    • With extra vans calculate having extras for the next day/s. do not send everything not in use out. Swap vans if needed to make sure vans are at station

    Notes with eli

    only have one long term van at the shop at a time(anything more than 2 days worth of work), & only one short term van like bent side/back steps. ask eli for time estimate if unsure to leave at the shop or not

    Notes with firestone

    (take down milage before heading in) try to only have one long term and one short term van at a time. tires should get done but for now do not prioritize. Anything engine-light related usually takes days to get back. Tiers can usually get done same day or next day in the morning . Ask firestone for time estimate if unsure (you can call and ask for an update, say you have more vans to bring but want to time things out right)

    Naperville dealership notes

    Anything that feels like an axle problem or transmission problem is a dealership problem . firestone will tell you guys if its a warranty problem after they check it at that point find time to move it over to the dealership (bring firestone Check engine light vans)

    Extra Notes
    • Headlight bulbs (H7): Oreilys, Advanced auto Parts, Autozone
    • Coolant/ Windshield wiper fluid: Blains
